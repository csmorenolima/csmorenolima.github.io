Project snapshot

A product ecosystem for safer access to menstrual care.

ENOVA responds to period poverty and stigma in education by pairing discreet physical access points with supportive digital guidance.

Audience
Students in UK education
Purpose
Access, dignity and wellbeing
System
Physical and digital artefacts
Method
Human-centred product design
Academic project context

From research proposal to engineered artefact.

The work was developed across two connected modules. Research Methods for Design Projects established the subject, project brief, work plan and preliminary justification. The Product Design Project then required that proposal to be developed from concept through to a fully resolved physical and/or digital artefact.

The story

Designing for confidence, privacy and everyday use.

ENOVA proposes a combination of physical products and a dedicated application for educational institutions. The goal is to empower and support young people by tackling period poverty and reducing the stigma around menstruation.

The concept uses soft, calm visual language and approachable product touchpoints so that practical care feels normal, accessible and safe.

500 million

women and girls are living in period poverty every month.

Stigma

around menstruation can create silence, discomfort and barriers to support.

10%

of girls use newspapers, rags, toilet paper and leaves as substitutes for sanitary products.

1 in 10

girls in the UK in 2017 could not afford to buy menstrual products.

Solution system

Four touchpoints, one support experience.

Each element is designed to reduce friction: access products, carry them discreetly, verify availability and support menstrual education.

ENOVA Dispenser

A school-based access point for menstrual products, designed to feel intentional, visible and non-stigmatising.

Protective Case

A compact carry solution that keeps products clean, private and easy to store in everyday school life.

Student Card

A simple access and identification layer that fits naturally into an educational environment.

ENOVA App

A digital companion for product guidance, catalogue browsing and educational support around menstrual wellbeing.
